About

About.

Paige Monet Brown is the Principal and Founder of Jefferson & Associates LLC, a Dallas-based regulatory strategy and government affairs firm. A citizen of the Mvskoke Creek Nation (Creek Tribe of Oklahoma) and a decade-long veteran of some of the most politically complex regulatory environments in the country, she brings rare political intelligence, coalition discipline, and community trust to every engagement.

She has directed Farm Bill legislative strategy, engaged the House Agriculture Committee on federal hemp reform, and coordinated public comment campaigns that set engagement-volume records at ODA and OLCC — contributing to the defeat of adverse legislation in Oregon in the process. She built and governs three national advocacy organizations including ORHAA, Oregon's leading cannabis coalition, and has structured $14.5M in advocacy funding strategy across eight states. A UT Austin graduate with dual degrees in African & African Diaspora Studies and Liberal Arts, she is an alumna of the Los Angeles African American Women in Public Policy program (2021) and a regular presence at MJBizCon, CHAMPS, and federal fly-ins in Washington, D.C.

Before founding Jefferson & Associates, Paige led PMB Consulting in Los Angeles, where she built a reputation as a go-to strategist for high-stakes cannabis licensing, land entitlement, and community engagement. Her work with developers, municipalities, and social equity entrepreneurs helped shape California's post-legalization industry — translating city and county regulations into actionable pathways for operators in historically excluded communities, with agency experience spanning LA City Planning, LADBS, LA County Health, and the City of Los Angeles Department of Cannabis Regulation.

As a queer, Black, and Indigenous woman, Paige has built her practice on the conviction that the communities most affected by regulatory decisions deserve a seat at the table where those decisions are made. Based in Dallas, TX, she is happily married to her husband and Jefferson & Associates co-founder, Drew Hull. Beyond her professional life, she is a performer and theatremaker, a dedicated horror film enthusiast, and devoted dog mom to a Yorkie named Jefferson.

What I Do

Expertise.

I.

Federal Hemp & Farm Bill Policy

Directing legislative strategy tied to Farm Bill reform, tracking House Agriculture Committee activity, and designing national campaigns to revise the federal definition of hemp across regulatory and congressional channels.

Result — Contributed to HB 4139 and SB 1548 failing to advance in Oregon; active legislative monitoring across eight states.

II.

Government Affairs & Lobbying

Representing clients at federal and state levels — direct engagement with Congress, executive branch, and agency staff across Oregon, Texas, Ohio, Florida, Arizona, Washington, Maryland, and New York.

Result — $14.5M in advocacy funding strategy structured; direct engagement with the House Agriculture Committee on Farm Bill reform.

III.

Coalition & Association Management

Formation, governance, and ongoing management of trade associations and advocacy organizations — including ORHAA, America First Agriculture Action Inc., and the American Healthy Alternatives Association.

Result — Built ORHAA into Oregon's largest hemp and cannabis trade association; governance structures built for three national 501(c)(4) organizations from inception.

IV.

Strategic Communications & Public Affairs

Messaging playbooks, rapid response frameworks, rulemaking comment coordination, and public affairs campaigns that position clients as credible, proactive voices in high-stakes policy environments.

Result — Coordinated public comment campaign achieving the highest single-rulemaking engagement volume on record for OLCC.

V.

Land Use, Licensing & Permitting

Site selection, zoning analysis, entitlement support, and agency navigation across dispensaries, consumption lounges, affordable housing, and mixed-use developments — with deep experience across Los Angeles County agencies.

Result — 40+ cannabis and mixed-use licensing and entitlement projects across LA County — navigating City Planning, LADBS, LA County Health, and the City DCR.

VI.

Organizational Structuring & Governance

501(c)(4) and PAC structuring, D&O insurance procurement, bylaw development, board operations, compliance frameworks, and financial systems for advocacy organizations built to withstand regulatory scrutiny.

Result — National 501(c)(4) and PAC structures formed and operationalized with D&O insurance, compliance systems, and financial frameworks in place.
